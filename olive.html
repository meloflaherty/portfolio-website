Olive

Focusing on Health, Wellness & Fitness

Olive is a responsive mobile app directed toward health-conscious users to give easy access to their health portal, ways to learn more about wellness and conveniently join fitness classes. This project showcases my start to finish process including research, wireframing, testing and an interactive prototype using Figma.

Overall Timeline: 9 months

In designing Olive, my goal was to create something inclusive, fun and easy to use! Here is my process.

Empathize

Problem: Health and wellness continue to influence the young professionals who are trying to achieve a good "work-life" balance. Users need an app that will hold them accountable by keeping track of upcoming medical appointments, booking fitness classes and other general health and well being services.

Solution: Olive will allow users to input medical information, schedule dr. appointments, add reminders of upcoming or past due appointments. Building incentive by incorporating challenges to enter, fitness classes to book and easy access to health and wellness blogs while on the go!

Research

Competitive Analysis

To begin, I took a look at some of the apps already on the market that are similar to Olive. I did a competitive analysis of the apps Mindbody & Apple Health. This helped me gain insight to what is already out there. What advantages or disadvantages each of them have to help me with my design process for Olive.

MindBody

Key Objectives: Your workout, Your way. This a free mobile app that makes it easy for all levels to schedule or drop-in to yoga classes, workout classes as well as after workout treatments such as massages, cryotherapy and/or beauty appointments.

Apple Health

Key Objectives: A secure and central place to store your health and fitness information. Receive highlights and create personalized sleep, fitness and duration schedules to keep track of and monitor your progression.

Interviews & Surveys

Conducting the user interviews & surveys my goal was to get an understanding as to what is looked for or appreciated in this type of app. I recruited 4 users to participate. With their permission, I recorded these sessions so I could review and take notes on the quotes and observations from the interviews. I created an affinity map to organize the behaviors, attitudes, needs, goals, frustrations and quotes.

Ideate

Personas & Journey Maps

I created 2 user personas, this gave me the sense of designing wants and needs for an actual user. I created 2 personas who would be utilizing the app for different reasons. Creating two personas helped me determine features that were being designed for each of them, this helped me not lose sight of that. Once I had the personas created, I made a journey map to be able to visually see what they would experience when trying to complete certain tasks. I got a better understanding as to where frustrations may come from and better insight as to what would streamline their experience.

User Flows & Sitemaps

Now that I have users I am designing for, I created user flows. This will help to map the process and show how they will complete these tasks on the Olive app.

Card Sorting

I also utilized the card sorting method. I had participants group together key aspects of the app to show me where they'd typically look for or think to find this feature within Olive. I created a sitemap to help visualize where all the features could be found based on those results.

Prototype

Low-fidelity sketches

I began the design process of my 3 key features starting with low-fidelity sketches.

Sign-up process

  • I was trying to keep it simple and to the point. An extensive questionnaire can cause users to shy away from completing the sign up.

Book a fitness class

  • Users are able to search by location or class type to easily work it into their schedule depending on their restrictions.

View documents

  • I wanted my user to feel that their personal information was safely stored in Olive, users will be prompted with a secondary passcode for security.

Using Figma, I turned my sketches into clickable prototypes. I conducted usability testing to help with the progression of my design. Taking into consideration all the information I've gathered thus far.

Usability Testing

Goal: The goal of this study was to assess the learnability for new users interacting with the application for the first time on their mobile device. I wanted to observe and measure if users understood the app and how they interacted with the tasks.

Test Objectives

  • Observe how easy and quick the user can log in or create an account
  • How does the user interact with the search by keyword and location, as well as the sort or view by feature
  • Determine if the user has any issue or confusion with the secondary passcode that protects their medical documents
